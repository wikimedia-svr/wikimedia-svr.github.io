“Where a direct quotation is attributed to a named, living individual in any article receiving more than 2,000 pageviews within a 72-hour window, the Verification Task Force may issue a Source Verification Request (SVR) requiring the cited individual to confirm, amend, or retract the attributed statement. Compliance requirements

To resolve this SVR, the cited individual (Carla Zastrow) must provide the following within the compliance window:

  • Signed digital or physical affidavit confirming the attributed quotation is accurate and was provided voluntarily. Acceptable formats: scanned PDF, photograph of signed document, or e-signature via DocuSign or equivalent. The affidavit must include the statement: “I, [full name], confirm that the quotation attributed to me in the Wikipedia article ‘Olive (cat)’ is accurate and was provided of my own free will.”
  • Photographic evidence of interaction with the subject. At minimum, one (1) photograph clearly depicting the cited individual in the presence of the article’s subject (Olive). This is required under SAIP §4.7.2(c) to establish the source’s “direct and demonstrable familiarity” with the entity described.
  • Brief biographical statement (2–3 sentences) establishing the cited individual’s qualifications to comment on the subject. This may include, but is not limited to: duration of relationship with subject, frequency of interaction, and any relevant expertise in feline behavior or temperament assessment.
